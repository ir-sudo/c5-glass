What we do

Here's how we catch them.

Most glass-shop sites were built by a family member on GoDaddy who never thought about getting found on Google — so they barely rank. It's not that the phone never rings. It's that the calls you catch are the onesies and twosies — a hack-out, a mirror, a tabletop — the small jobs you happened to be free to grab. The bigger, more profitable work goes to whoever shows up first in the search. Yours is built to do a job: four working parts that get you found for the jobs that pay, get you quoting first, and catch every call you can't pick up.

Show up on Google for the work you want

Dedicated service-area and service-line pages so you rank for "frameless shower [your city]" and "storefront glass [your city]" — the high-intent searches you're invisible for today. Plus every photo of your work labeled so your showers and storefronts show up in Google image search, instead of sitting on a page nobody finds.

See an example page →

Quote first, win the job

Not every job gets an instant number — a storefront isn't a shower swap, and we'd never pretend it is. So we review your business first and find the work that can be quoted fast — mirrors, shower swaps, glass replacement — and build a request flow that lets customers ask in seconds and you answer first. The bigger, custom jobs route to a smart intake that captures the details and photos up front, so you quote faster without the back-and-forth.

Catch the calls you can't pick up

When a call comes in and you're on a job, it doesn't have to roll to voicemail. We catch it for you — an assistant can answer right away, or an automatic text goes straight back to the customer within seconds. Either way they hear from you first, instead of hanging up and calling the next shop on the list.

Built for the phone first, not the desktop

Most searches happen on a phone — and most owners have never opened their own site on one. To the customer, that screen is the whole experience, so we build it phone-first: one tap calls you, they see your work, they send a quote request right there. Fewer small calls, more time for the big jobs.
